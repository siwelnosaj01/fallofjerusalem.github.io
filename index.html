The Fall of Jerusalem

Like no other event in history.

Next

"In a good deal of Christian theology the fall of Jerusalem has had no theological significance."
-N.T. Wright

For many years past a copy of the Jewish Wars as told by the first century Jewish historian Josephus could be found alongside the Bible in many homes. The details of the destruction of Jerusalem and the end of the Jewish state under Rome is one of the clearest indication apart from the gospels that Jesus of Nazareth is a true prophet.

But more than a prophet...

that Jesus foretold accurately about the destruction of Jerusalem, establishes all of his other claims as valid: his resurrection, the global spread of his church, that he will return.

Let the fall of Jerusalem in 70AD return to theological signficance as the return of Christ is nearer than ever.

The Fall of Jerusalem a prelude to greater catastrophe yet to come

Jesus predicted the fall of Jerusalem. Jesus also predicted the end of human history altogether and a time of judgement that will come upon the whole earth. To remember the fall of Jerusalem, is to remember the prophecies of Jesus are not yet complete. A warning was given then, as a warning is given to us now.
